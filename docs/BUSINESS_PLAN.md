## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Business Concept

**SupportCarr** is an on-demand roadside rescue platform specifically designed for micro-mobility users (e-bikes, bicycles, cargo bikes). Operating as "AAA for cyclists," SupportCarr provides emergency assistance when riders experience mechanical issues, flat tires, dead batteries, or need transport to a safe location.

### 1.2 Mission Statement

To create the most reliable, transparent, and equitable micro-mobility support network that prioritizes rider safety, driver prosperity, and environmental sustainability.

### 1.3 Key Success Factors

- **Speed:** Average wait time ≤8 minutes (industry-leading)
- **Reliability:** 95%+ completion rate at scale
- **Fair Economics:** 75% revenue share for drivers (vs. 50-60% industry standard)
- **Transparency:** Public impact metrics (CO₂e avoided, uptime, completion rates)
- **Compliance-First:** Proactive regulatory engagement and safety protocols

### 1.4 Financial Highlights

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **MVP Investment** | $583,000 |
| **Break-Even Point** | 6,000 rides (16-20 weeks) |
| **Year 1 Revenue Projection** | $2,400,000 |
| **Expected ROI** | 240% |
| **Target Launch Revenue** | 40 rides/day @ $50 avg ticket |

### 1.5 Funding Requirement

Seeking **$400,000 pre-seed** to fund:
- Santa Monica/Echo Park pilot operations
- Driver acquisition and training
- Technical infrastructure hardening
- Regulatory compliance and insurance
- Initial decentralized battery-swap locker prototype

### 2.2 Problem Statement

The micro-mobility revolution has created millions of e-bike and bicycle riders who face critical gaps:

1. **Safety & Stranding:** Riders stranded by mechanical failures, flat tires, or dead batteries in unsafe locations
2. **Downtime:** Delivery workers lose income during repair/charging downtime (3+ hours for battery charge)
3. **Charging Compliance:** Rising battery fire incidents driving regulatory crackdowns on home/workplace charging
4. **Lack of Infrastructure:** No systematic roadside assistance network for non-motorized vehicles
5. **Environmental Impact:** Stranded riders resorting to ride-sharing or vehicle purchases

### 2.3 Solution

SupportCarr provides:

- **Immediate Rescue:** On-demand assistance with 8-minute average response
- **Multiple Service Types:** Flat repair, mechanical assist, battery towing, bike recovery, optional rider transport
- **Driver-Friendly Platform:** Industry-leading 75% revenue share, transparent earnings, safety training
- **Technology-Enabled Matching:** Geospatial AI matching nearest qualified driver to rider
- **Future Battery Infrastructure:** Decentralized solar-powered battery swap network

**Roadside Assistance Market:**
- **AAA Membership:** 64M members paying $60-120/year
- **Total Market:** $28B annually (automotive-focused)

## 4. Products and Services

### 4.1 Core Service: On-Demand Rescue (MVP)

#### Service Types

**1. Flat Tire Assistance**
- **What:** On-site tire repair or tube replacement
- **Duration:** 10-20 minutes
- **Equipment:** Patch kit, spare tubes (common sizes), pump, levers
- **Pricing:** $50 base (pilot flat rate)

**2. Mechanical Assist**
- **What:** Chain issues, brake adjustments, minor repairs
- **Duration:** 15-30 minutes
- **Equipment:** Multi-tool, chain breaker, lubricant
- **Pricing:** $50 base (pilot flat rate)

**3. Dead Battery Towing**
- **What:** Transport e-bike with dead battery to destination or charging location
- **Duration:** 10-40 minutes (depending on distance)
- **Equipment:** Bike rack or cargo trailer
- **Pricing:** $50 base (pilot); future dynamic pricing

**4. Bike-Only Recovery**
- **What:** Transport bike to safe location (rider arranges separate transport)
- **Duration:** 15-45 minutes
- **Equipment:** Secure bike rack
- **Pricing:** $50 base (pilot)

**5. Full Recovery (Bike + Rider)**
- **What:** Optional transport of both bike and rider to destination
- **Duration:** 20-60 minutes
- **Equipment:** Vehicle with passenger capacity + bike rack
- **Pricing:** $60-80 (higher insurance/licensing requirements)

### 4.4 Decentralized Battery-Swap Infrastructure (Phase 3 - Month 19+)

**SupportCarr Swap Network**

**Concept:**
Weather-sealed, solar-powered battery swap lockers deployed at high-density nodes (transit hubs, delivery zones, tourist corridors).

**Components:**
- **Locker Units:** OSHA/NEC-compliant, fire-rated enclosures
- **Power:** Solar primary, grid backup, remote monitoring
- **Access:** Mobile app unlocking, subscription or PAYG
- **Maintenance:** Weekly courier servicing, predictive monitoring

**Use Cases:**
1. **Delivery Worker Mid-Shift Swap:** Replace dead battery in 2 minutes vs. 3-hour charge
2. **Tourist Corridors:** Joshua Tree, PCH, rural bike routes
3. **Urban Hubs:** Transit stations, commercial districts

**Business Model:**
- **Swap Fee:** $8-12 per swap (vs. 3-hour downtime)
- **Membership:** $49/month unlimited swaps (for delivery workers)
- **B2B:** Fleet operator contracts for dedicated capacity

**Target Deployment:**
- Year 2: 20 lockers in LA/NYC pilot zones
- Year 3: 100 lockers across 5 cities
- Year 5: 500+ lockers (national network)

**Public Transparency:**
- Real-time uptime dashboard (SupportCarr.com/swapstatus)
- CO₂e avoided calculations (vs. gas vehicle trips)
- Community access API for civic apps

### 5.2 Pricing Strategy

#### Dynamic Pricing Model (Post-Pilot)

**Base Rate Calculation:**
- Distance-based: $30 base + $2/mile
- Service type multiplier: Flat tire (1.0x), Mechanical (1.2x), Towing (1.5x)
- Surge bounds: 1.0x - 3.0x (transparent, capped)

**Surge Triggers:**
- Supply/demand ratio < 0.5 (more requests than available drivers)
- Weather conditions (rain, extreme heat)
- Time of day (late night premium)
- Zone density (high-demand areas)

**Surge Mitigation:**
- Membership dampening (50-75% reduction for members)
- B2B contracts exempt from surge
- Floor pricing for safety-critical situations

**Transparency:**
- Upfront quote before booking
- Surge multiplier displayed clearly
- Historical pricing data public (trust-building)

## 9. Risk Analysis and Mitigation

### 9.1 Market Risks

#### Risk: Insufficient Rider Demand

**Likelihood:** Medium
**Impact:** High

**Mitigation:**
- Willingness-to-Pay surveys already showing 68% willing to pay $40-60
- Existing informal services charge $80-120 (price gap validation)
- Free trial campaigns to drive initial adoption
- B2B contracts provide baseline demand floor

#### Risk: Supply Shortage (Driver Recruitment)

**Likelihood:** Medium
**Impact:** High

**Mitigation:**
- 75% revenue share (industry-leading, compelling value prop)
- Over-recruit by 30% to account for churn
- Guaranteed minimums during peak hours (income security)
- Equipment stipends and training reduce barriers to entry
- Referral bonuses ($100/driver) for network effects

#### Risk: Competition from Incumbents (Uber/AAA)

**Likelihood:** Medium (long-term)
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- First-mover advantage in micro-mobility focus (brand equity)
- Superior driver economics create supply stickiness
- Community trust and transparency (vs. corporate)
- Niche expertise (battery, e-bike specific) hard to replicate
- Potential acquirer (strategic partnership vs. competition)

### 9.2 Operational Risks

#### Risk: Safety Incidents (Driver or Rider Injury)

**Likelihood:** Low
**Impact:** High

**Mitigation:**
- Comprehensive driver safety training (certification required)
- Insurance coverage (platform liability + driver occupational accident)
- Scene safety protocols (arrival checklist, escalation procedures)
- Incident response playbook (legal, PR, support)
- Background checks and ongoing monitoring

#### Risk: Battery Fire During Transport

**Likelihood:** Low
**Impact:** Extreme

**Mitigation:**
- Driver training on lithium-ion risks (thermal runaway signs)
- Transport protocols (external racks, not enclosed cargo)
- Insurance riders specifically for battery transport
- Collaboration with fire departments (best practices)
- Exclude damaged/punctured batteries from service (visual inspection)

#### Risk: Service Level Failures (Long Wait Times)

**Likelihood:** Medium (early)
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Over-recruit drivers (30% buffer above minimum)
- Auto-reoffer with expanded radius (algorithmic fallback)
- Supervisor escalation for SLA breach risk (manual dispatch)
- Transparent ETA communication (manage expectations)
- Refund policy for excessive delays (goodwill)

### 9.3 Financial Risks

#### Risk: Unit Economics Don't Improve with Scale

**Likelihood:** Low
**Impact:** High

**Mitigation:**
- Current 15% contribution margin sufficient for break-even at scale
- Membership and B2B improve margins (higher LTV, lower CAC)
- Dynamic pricing post-pilot allows margin optimization
- Fixed cost leverage (ops team supports 10x rides with 2x headcount)
- Battery swap and utility marketplace add high-margin revenue streams

#### Risk: Payment Fraud or Chargebacks

**Likelihood:** Medium
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Stripe fraud detection (machine learning models)
- Pre-authorization hold (not captured until completion)
- Photo evidence required (driver arrival, completion)
- Dispute playbook with evidence submission
- Ban repeat offenders from platform

#### Risk: Insurance Cost Escalation

**Likelihood:** Medium
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Partner with insurers early (Pie, Buckle, gig-focused)
- Safety record and training reduce premiums over time
- Self-insurance fund for minor claims (reserve)
- Pass-through fees to pricing if necessary (transparency)
- Explore captive insurance structure at scale

### 9.4 Regulatory Risks

#### Risk: Classification Uncertainty (TNC vs. Courier vs. Roadside)

**Likelihood:** Medium
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Early legal counsel (transportation law specialist)
- Proactive engagement with city regulators (MoUs)
- Clear ToS and independent contractor agreements
- Monitor AB5 (CA) and similar legislation
- Flexible business model (can adapt to employee classification if required)

#### Risk: Battery Charging/Storage Regulation

**Likelihood:** Medium (high in NYC)
**Impact:** Medium

**Mitigation:**
- Align with Local Law 39 (NYC) and fire codes
- Certified charging/storage training for drivers

- Position as regulatory compliance partner (not adversary)
- Public safety messaging (reduce home charging fires)

#### Risk: Licensing Requirements (Per Jurisdiction)

**Likelihood:** Low

   - **Timing:** Year 5-6 (post NYC scale, pre national expansion)

   - **Rationale:** Extend service portfolio to non-automotive segment
   - **Valuation:** $30-60M (strategic premium for membership model)
   - **Timing:** Year 5-7 (proven unit economics, membership base)

3. **Delivery Platforms (DoorDash, UberEats, Instacart)**
   - **Rationale:** Reduce courier downtime, improve worker retention
   - **Valuation:** $50-100M (strategic value to workforce ops)
   - **Timing:** Year 4-6 (B2B contracts prove integration value)

4. **E-bike OEMs (Rad Power, Bosch, Specialized)**
   - **Rationale:** Create differentiated service network (Apple Care for e-bikes)
   - **Valuation:** $20-40M (smaller strategic, brand value)
   - **Timing:** Year 6-8 (battery swap network as moat)

**Acquisition Triggers:**
- Revenue: $10-20M ARR
- Markets: 5-10 cities with density
- Battery swap network: 100+ lockers operational
- B2B contracts: 15-20 enterprise customers
- Membership base: 5,000-10,000 active subscribers

#### Path 2: Independent Growth to IPO (10+ Years) - **15% Probability**

**Scenario:**
- Scale to 25+ major cities nationally
- $200M+ ARR (rescue + battery swap + utility)
- 50,000+ active drivers
- 1,000+ battery swap lockers
- Profitability at scale (20%+ EBITDA margins)

**IPO Valuation:** $500M - $1B (3-5x revenue multiple)

**Challenges:**
- Requires Series B ($10M+), Series C ($30M+) fundraising

**Likelihood:** Lower probability due to capital requirements and competitive dynamics, but viable if early traction is exceptional.

#### Path 3: Sustainable Independent (Indefinite) - **20% Probability**

**Scenario:**
- Remain regional powerhouse (LA + NYC)

**Scenario:**

SupportCarr represents a unique opportunity at the intersection of micro-mobility growth, gig economy evolution, and urban decarbonization. With a **production-ready MVP, validated demand, and compelling unit economics**, the company is positioned to become the dominant on-demand rescue platform for cyclists and e-bike riders.

1. **Massive Underserved Market:** 50M+ cyclists in the US with zero organized roadside assistance

4. **Defensible Moat:** Driver economics (75% revenue share), community trust, battery/e-bike specialization
5. **Multiple Revenue Streams:** Per-job fees, memberships, B2B contracts, battery swap, utility marketplace
6. **Clear Path to Profitability:** Break-even at 6,000 rides (Month 6), profitable by Year 3
